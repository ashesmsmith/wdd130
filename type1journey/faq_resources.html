FAQ

Q: What is the normal number for blood sugar?

A: 100 give or take 20 points depending on when and what was last ate.


Q: Do you have to change your diet? No more sugar?

A: We don't have to change the diet as much as most people think. They can still have sugar, in fact carbohydrates convert to 'sugars' that the body needs for energy. As long as they dose the right amount of insulin for what they are eating, they can have almost anything. The biggest exception is sugary drinks unless they are low and need to come back up.


Q: What is DKA?

A: Diabetic Ketoacidosis. It can be life-threatening if not treated correctly and quickly.


Q: How did you know to check Tyler's blood sugar before diagnosis?

Q: How are Type 1 and Type 2 different?

A: Type 1 is an autoimmune disease with no cure at this time. The pancreas has stopped producing insulin (or will soon) which is essential for the body. It is not brought on by lifestyle or eating too much sugar.

Type 2 is the most common and is not an autoimmune disease. It varies in degrees of severity and is often (but not always) caused by lifestyle and diet. If caught early enough, it be treated with medication and/or diet. Sometimes it even goes away! Only some Type 2 patients require insulin whereas all Type 1 patients do or will require insulin.
