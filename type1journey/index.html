Our Journey with Type 1 Diabetes

How It All Started

Welcome! We are the Smith Family. Derek (dad), Ashley (mom), Tyler and Elizabeth. We created this page to share our journey and life living with Type 1 Diabetes. Derek and Tyler are the Type 1 diabetics in our family but it affects us all in different ways.

Our journey with Type 1 Diabetes started in June 2021, or April 2017, depending on how you look at it. More on that in a minute.

In June 2021, Derek and I began to notice symptoms of diabetes in our son. He was starving and thirsty no matter how much he ate or drank, and he started having frequent accidents at night which had never been a problem for him before. We originally thought the accidents were because he was drinking so much, and he was drinking a lot because it was a hot summer, and he must be eating a lot because he was growing. After about a week of this, we cut him off from water at around 6pm. Not long after he started crying because his mouth felt dry and he was so thirsty. That's when realized it must be something more. Derek had been diagnosed with Type 2 diabetes back in April 2017 and his biggest symptom was excessive thirst. That night we decided to test Tyler's blood sugar using Derek's meter. It read as 553! The normal range is about 100 (give or take 20 points). We immediately headed to the emergency room where they were about to test his blood sugar and a1c, as well as give him some insulin. He was still 387 when we left the hospital but we had an appointment with a pediatric endocrinologist for the next day. Fast forward a couple months, after learning A TON of information about Type 1 Diabetes (there's so much more to learn still) and learning the differences between Type 1 and Type 2, I began to get suspicious that Derek had been misdiagnosed because of his age. Type 1 typically occurs in children and is often referred to as Juvenile Diabetes. Derek was 30 when he was diagnosed. We decided to get him checked by a specialist and sure enough in October 2021 he received an official diagnosis of Type 1 Diabetes (just a late onset). It was a miracle that he didn't have any major complications from being diagnosed incorrectly as the treatment for these types is very different.
